# Federal Standards Reference

This file consolidates the primary federal standards referenced across
all five pillars. Each entry notes its current status as of May 2026.

## NIST Publications

**NIST AI RMF 1.0** (January 26, 2023) — AI Risk Management Framework.
Four functions: GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE. Current and operative.

**NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5** (September 2020, updated December 2020) —
Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations.
Current. The SR family added in Rev 5 covers supply-chain risk.

**NIST SP 800-160 Vol 1 Rev 1** (November 2022) — Engineering Trustworthy
Secure Systems. Current.

**NIST SP 800-160 Vol 2 Rev 1** (December 2021) — Developing
Cyber-Resilient Systems. Contains 14 cyber-resiliency techniques.
Current.

**NIST SP 800-161 Rev 1 upd1** (November 2024) — Cybersecurity
Supply Chain Risk Management. Current.

**NIST SP 800-207** (August 2020) — Zero Trust Architecture. Seven
tenets. Current.

**NIST SP 800-207A** (September 2023) — A Zero Trust Architecture Model
for Access Control in Cloud-Native Systems. Current.

**NIST SP 1800-35** (final June 2025) — Implementing a Zero Trust
Architecture. 19 reference implementations from 24 industry
collaborators. Current.

**NIST Privacy Framework 1.0** (January 2020) — Current. Version 1.1
IPD published April 2025.

## OMB Memoranda

**OMB M-22-09** (January 26, 2022) — Moving the U.S. Government Toward
Zero Trust Cybersecurity Principles. FY24 deadline extended via NSM-8
for DoD/IC. Current.

**Note:** M-22-18 and M-23-16 were rescinded in early 2026, weakening
centralized supply-chain attestation requirements at exactly the moment
AI supply-chain risk surged.

## CISA Guidance

**CISA Secure-by-Design** (April 2023 white paper; updated 2024).
RSA Pledge (May 8, 2024) — 250+ signatories by late 2025. Current.

**CISA Zero Trust Maturity Model 2.0** (April 2023) — Five pillars ×
four maturity stages × three cross-cutting capabilities. Current.

**CISA August 2024 Joint Event-Logging Guide** — Current.

**CISA 2025 Minimum Elements for an SBOM NPRM** (August 22, 2025) —
Proposed rule. Not yet final as of May 2026.

## Executive Orders

**EO 14028** (May 12, 2021) — Improving the Nation's Cybersecurity.
SBOM lineage via NTIA July 2021 minimum elements. Current.

## DoD Guidance

**DoD Zero Trust Strategy** (November 2022) — FY27 Target / FY32
Advanced, 152 capabilities. Current.

**DTM 25-003** (effective July 17, 2025) — Codifies the EXCOM and
Chief Zero Trust Officer. Current.

## Procurement Regulations

**FAR 52.204-21** — Basic Safeguarding of Covered Contractor Information.
Current.

**FAR 52.204-25** — Section 889 prohibition. Current.

**DFARS 252.204-7012** — Safeguarding Covered Defense Information;
72-hour cyber-incident reporting; mandatory subcontract flow-down.
Current.

**DFARS 252.204-7021** — CMMC 2.0 requirement. Final September 10, 2025,
effective November 10, 2025, phased over four years to November 10, 2028.
Current.

**CMMC 2.0** (32 CFR Part 170) — Final October 15, 2024, effective
December 16, 2024. Levels: L1 (FCI self-assessment), L2 (CUI
third-party assessment by C3PAO), L3 (high CUI government-led DIBCAC).

## International Standards

**ISO/IEC 42001** (December 2023) — AI Management System Standard.
Current.

**EU AI Act** (Regulation 2024/1689) — Provider/deployer split.
Article 53 GPAI obligations operative since August 2, 2025. Article 88
enforcement vests in the AI Office; penalties up to 7% of global
turnover.

**GPAI Code of Practice** — Finalized July 10, 2025.
